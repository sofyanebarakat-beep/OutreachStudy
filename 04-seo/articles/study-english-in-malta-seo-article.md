## Why Study English in Malta?

Malta has built a strong reputation as an English study destination because it combines language learning with a student-friendly lifestyle. English is widely used in education, business, tourism, and everyday services, which means students can practise beyond the classroom.

The country is especially attractive for students who want:

- English immersion in Europe
- A warmer climate than the UK or Ireland
- Short-term and long-term course options
- International classmates from many countries
- Flexible start dates throughout the year
- A smaller destination that is easier to navigate

Malta also has a regulated English language teaching sector. The [ELT Council Malta](https://eltcouncil.gov.mt/) oversees standards for English language teaching, and organizations such as [FELTOM](https://feltom.com/) represent accredited English language schools. This matters because students should choose a licensed school, not only the cheapest offer.

## Who Is Malta Best For?

Malta can work well for many types of international students, but it is especially strong for students who want practical communication skills.

### Beginners and Intermediate Learners

If you need more confidence speaking English, Malta gives you daily opportunities to practise. You can use English in class, in shops, in cafes, on public transport, and during activities with other students.

### Students Preparing for University or Work

Many students choose Malta before starting higher education, applying for jobs, or preparing for an international move. A focused English course can help with speaking, listening, writing, and academic confidence.

### Professionals

Business English and intensive courses can suit professionals who want to improve workplace communication, presentations, meetings, emails, and interviews.

### Students Who Want a Balanced Lifestyle

Malta is not only about studying. Students often choose it because they can enjoy the sea, historic cities, international events, and weekend activities while improving English.

## Types of English Courses in Malta

Most English schools in Malta offer several course types. The best choice depends on your level, budget, goals, and how quickly you want to improve.

### General English

General English is the most common option. It usually focuses on speaking, listening, reading, writing, grammar, vocabulary, and everyday communication.

This course is good if you want to:

- Improve overall English level
- Build confidence in conversation
- Study for several weeks or months
- Balance lessons with free time

### Intensive English

Intensive English includes more weekly lessons than a standard course. It is useful if you have limited time or want faster progress.

Choose intensive English if you:

- Need rapid improvement
- Have a clear deadline
- Want more classroom practice
- Prefer a structured schedule

### Business English

Business English is designed for students and professionals who need English for work. Lessons may include meetings, presentations, negotiations, emails, and professional vocabulary.

### Exam Preparation

Some students study English in Malta to prepare for IELTS, Cambridge exams, or other language tests. Exam preparation courses can be helpful if you need a specific score for university, immigration, or professional purposes.

### One-to-One Lessons

Private lessons are more expensive but highly focused. They can be useful if you have a specific weakness, such as pronunciation, academic writing, interview practice, or business communication.

## How to Choose the Right English School in Malta

Choosing a school is not only about the weekly price. A cheaper course may not be the best value if the location, timetable, class size, or support services do not match your needs.

Before applying, check these points:

- Is the school licensed or accredited?
- What levels are available?
- How many students are usually in each class?
- Does the school offer a placement test?
- Are course materials included?
- Is there an end-of-course certificate?
- Does the school help with visa documents?
- What accommodation options are available?
- Is the location convenient for transport and daily life?

It is also important to match the school to your personality. Some students prefer a large, social school with many activities. Others prefer a quieter environment with smaller classes and more personal attention.

## How Much Does It Cost to Study English in Malta?

The cost to study English in Malta depends on course intensity, season, accommodation type, and lifestyle. Summer is often more expensive because demand is higher. Longer courses may have better weekly rates.

Typical cost categories include:

- Course tuition
- Registration fee
- Course materials
- Accommodation
- Airport transfer
- Visa-related costs if applicable
- Health insurance
- Food and groceries
- Transport
- Personal spending

As a general planning range, many students should prepare for a monthly budget that includes both course and living costs. The exact amount depends heavily on whether you choose shared accommodation, a host family, a private room, or a more independent apartment.

### Budget Tips for Students

You can reduce costs by:

- Studying outside peak summer months
- Booking a longer course
- Choosing shared accommodation
- Cooking some meals at home
- Using public transport
- Comparing several school options before applying

## Accommodation Options for English Students in Malta

Accommodation can have a major impact on your student experience. The right choice depends on your budget, independence, and comfort level.

### Student Residence

Student residences are popular because they make it easy to meet other students. They can be a good choice if you want a social environment and a simple setup.

### Shared Apartment

Shared apartments are common for longer stays. They give students more independence, but you should check location, transport, bills, and contract conditions carefully.

### Host Family

A host family can be useful if you want more English practice and a supportive home environment. It may also include meals, depending on the package.

### Private Studio or Apartment

Private accommodation gives more privacy but usually costs more. It may be suitable for professionals, couples, or students who need a quiet study environment.

Before booking, compare travel time to school, included bills, kitchen access, laundry, Wi-Fi, deposit rules, and check-in arrangements. Our [student accommodation in Malta](https://outreachstudy.eu/student-accommodation-malta/) page can help you compare options.

## Malta Student Visa Basics

Visa rules depend on your nationality and course length. EU citizens have different rules from third-country nationals, and visa-exempt students may still need to apply if they plan to study for longer periods.

For many non-EU students:

- Courses not exceeding 90 days may fall under short-stay Schengen visa rules.
- Courses exceeding 90 days usually require a national long-stay visa.
- Students should prepare documents carefully before booking travel.
- Visa procedures and requirements can change, so always check official guidance.

[Identita](https://identita.gov.mt/central-visa-unit-sec-page-student-visa/) provides official information for student visas, including separate guidance for [courses not exceeding 90 days](https://identita.gov.mt/central-visa-unit-student-visa-courses-not-exceeding-90-days/) and [courses exceeding 90 days](https://identita.gov.mt/central-visa-unit-student-visa-courses-exceeding-90-days/). It also publishes information about [employment conditions for eligible students](https://identita.gov.mt/central-visa-unit-student-visa-employment/). For example, some students following courses longer than 90 days may be allowed to work up to 20 hours per week after meeting specific conditions, but this is subject to official rules.

Because visa mistakes can delay your plans, do not rely only on social media advice. Read our [Malta student visa guide](https://outreachstudy.eu/malta-student-visa-guide/) and confirm your personal situation before applying.

## Best Places to Study English in Malta

Malta is small, but location still matters. Your school area affects your commute, accommodation choices, social life, and daily budget.

### Sliema

Sliema is one of the most popular areas for international students. It has shops, cafes, seafront walks, transport connections, and many accommodation options.

### St Julian's

St Julian's is known for restaurants, nightlife, and a busy international atmosphere. It can suit students who want a lively social experience.

### Valletta

Valletta is Malta’s capital and a UNESCO World Heritage city. It is a good area for students who enjoy history, culture, and strong transport links.

### Gzira and Msida

These areas can be practical for students looking for more affordable accommodation while staying close to Sliema, Valletta, and major bus routes.

### Gozo

Gozo is quieter and more relaxed. It can suit students who prefer a slower pace, nature, and a smaller community feel.

## How Long Should You Study English in Malta?

Your ideal course length depends on your starting level and goal. A short course can improve confidence, but deeper progress usually needs more time and consistent practice.

A simple guide:

- 2 to 4 weeks: best for a language boost and travel experience
- 8 to 12 weeks: better for noticeable improvement
- 3 to 6 months: stronger option for serious progress
- 6 months or more: suitable for students preparing for higher education, work, or major life plans

Students who want a major level change should think beyond the classroom. Progress comes from attending lessons, doing homework, speaking daily, joining activities, and avoiding the habit of only socializing in your native language.

## 9. FAQ Section

### Is Malta a good place to study English?

Yes. Malta is a strong English study destination because English is an official language, the country has many international students, and learners can practise English in daily life as well as in class.

### How much does it cost to study English in Malta?

Costs vary by course, season, accommodation, and lifestyle. Students should budget for tuition, registration fees, accommodation, food, transport, insurance, and personal spending. Shared accommodation and off-season study can reduce the total cost.

### Do I need a visa to study English in Malta?

It depends on your nationality and course length. Non-EU students may need a short-stay visa for courses up to 90 days or a national long-stay visa for longer courses. Always check official guidance before applying.

### Can international students work while studying English in Malta?

Some students may be allowed to work under specific conditions, including course length and official permission rules. Identita states that certain eligible students may work up to 20 hours per week after meeting the relevant conditions, but rules can change.

### What is the best English course in Malta?

The best course depends on your goal. General English is good for overall improvement, intensive English is better for faster progress, Business English suits professionals, and exam preparation is best for students who need a specific score.
