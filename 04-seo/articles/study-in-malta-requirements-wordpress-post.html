What Are the Main Requirements to Study in Malta?

The requirements to study in Malta are usually divided into two groups: school admission requirements and visa requirements. Admission requirements are set by the school, college, university, or English language provider. Visa requirements are set by the Maltese authorities and depend mainly on your nationality and course duration.

Most international students should prepare for these requirement areas:

  • Valid passport
  • Course acceptance letter
  • Previous academic documents
  • English level proof, if required
  • Proof of funds
  • Proof of accommodation
  • Health or travel insurance
  • Visa application documents, if applicable
  • Payment proof for tuition or deposit
  • Residence permit documents for longer studies, where required

Academic Requirements for International Students

Academic requirements depend on the level of study. A short English course normally has simpler entry requirements than a university degree. A bachelor’s or master’s programme will usually require formal academic records and may ask for qualification recognition or course-specific documents.

English Language Courses

English language courses are usually flexible. Many schools accept students at different levels, from beginner to advanced. Instead of asking for previous certificates, the school may place you in the correct class after a placement test.

Common requirements may include:

  • Passport copy
  • Completed application form
  • Course selection and start date
  • Payment confirmation or deposit
  • Accommodation choice, if booked through the school
  • Visa documents for non-EU students, if needed

You can compare course types on our English courses in Malta page.

Foundation and Diploma Courses

Foundation and diploma courses often require proof that you completed secondary school or a previous level of study. Some programmes may also ask for English language proof, especially if the course is academic or professional.

You may need:

  • High school certificate or equivalent
  • Academic transcripts
  • English language certificate or placement test
  • Passport copy
  • Motivation statement for some courses
  • CV for professional programmes

Bachelor’s Degree Requirements

For bachelor’s degrees, students usually need to show that they completed secondary education at a level accepted by the institution. Requirements vary by course. Business, IT, health, and technical programmes may have different subject expectations.

Typical bachelor’s requirements may include:

  • High school certificate
  • Academic transcripts
  • English language proof
  • Passport copy
  • Motivation letter
  • Application form
  • Course deposit or tuition payment proof

Master’s Degree Requirements

For master’s degrees, students normally need a recognized bachelor’s degree or equivalent qualification. Some programmes may require a related academic background, professional experience, portfolio, interview, or stronger English level.

Typical master’s requirements may include:

  • Bachelor’s degree certificate
  • University transcripts
  • CV
  • Motivation letter or statement of purpose
  • Reference letters for some programmes
  • English language proof
  • Passport copy

For qualification recognition, Malta’s official MQRIC service under MFHEA provides information about how qualifications are compared with the Malta Qualifications Framework. You can review the official MFHEA academic qualifications guidance.

English Language Requirements

Because English is one of Malta’s official languages, many courses are taught in English. This is one of the biggest advantages for international students. However, the English requirement depends on the course type.

English language schools usually accept students at different levels and place them after a test. Higher education institutions may ask for IELTS, TOEFL, Cambridge English, Duolingo English Test, previous English-medium education, or an internal assessment. Each institution decides its own accepted evidence.

Before applying, check:

  • Minimum English score for your course
  • Whether the school accepts internal placement tests
  • Whether previous English-medium study is accepted
  • Whether you can start with an English preparation course
  • Whether your visa file needs English proof

Documents Needed to Apply

A complete file helps avoid delays. Even if requirements vary, most students should prepare a clean digital folder before applying.

Your application folder may include:

  • Passport scan
  • Recent passport-style photo
  • Academic certificates
  • Academic transcripts
  • English language certificate, if required
  • CV for professional or postgraduate courses
  • Motivation letter
  • Reference letters, if required
  • Course application form
  • Proof of payment or deposit

Documents that are not in English may need translation. Some institutions may also ask for certified copies. It is better to check this before submission because missing translations can slow down admission and visa preparation.

Malta Student Visa Requirements

Visa requirements depend on nationality and course duration. EU students and non-EU students usually follow different processes. Non-EU students should check official guidance before booking travel or paying non-refundable fees.

Official Identita guidance separates student visa information by course duration. For courses not exceeding 90 days, third-country nationals may need a Schengen short-stay C-visa, unless they are visa-exempt. For courses exceeding 90 days, applicants may need a national long-stay D-visa.

You can review the official Identita pages for student visa courses not exceeding 90 days and student visa courses exceeding 90 days.

Common visa requirements may include:

  • Valid passport
  • Completed visa application form
  • Acceptance letter from the school or institution
  • Proof of tuition payment or deposit
  • Proof of accommodation in Malta
  • Proof of sufficient funds
  • Health or travel insurance
  • Travel itinerary or booking information, if required
  • Passport photos
  • Supporting academic documents

Financial Requirements and Proof of Funds

Students need enough money to cover tuition, accommodation, food, transport, insurance, visa costs, and daily expenses. Proof of funds may be required by the school, the visa authority, or both.

Financial documents may include:

  • Recent bank statements
  • Sponsor letter, if someone supports you
  • Sponsor bank statements
  • Proof of tuition payment
  • Scholarship letter, if applicable
  • Accommodation payment proof, if already paid

The amount needed can depend on your stay length, accommodation type, and visa category. Always prepare more than the minimum if possible, because weak financial evidence can create delays or refusals.

Accommodation and Insurance Requirements

Many students must show where they will live in Malta. Accommodation proof may be needed for both the school and visa file. This can include a student residence booking, host family confirmation, shared apartment contract, or other accepted proof.

Before confirming accommodation, check:

  • Exact address
  • Distance from the school
  • Check-in and check-out dates
  • What bills are included
  • Deposit and cancellation rules
  • Whether the document is accepted for visa purposes

Health or travel insurance is also commonly required, especially for visa applications. The insurance should normally cover the full stay and meet the requirements for your visa type. Compare options before buying and keep the certificate ready.

Requirements for Short Courses vs Long Courses

The length of your course changes the requirements. A two-week English course, a 12-week language programme, and a one-year higher education course may have different visa and residence steps.

Courses Up to 90 Days

Courses up to 90 days may be simpler for some students, especially visa-exempt nationals. However, third-country nationals who need a visa may still need a Schengen short-stay C-visa and supporting documents.

Courses Over 90 Days

Courses over 90 days usually require more planning. According to official Identita guidance, applicants for courses exceeding 90 days may need a national long-stay D-visa. Depending on the course and stay length, students may also need to follow residence permit procedures after arrival.

For longer stays, timing matters. Prepare documents early and avoid booking travel before your visa path is clear.

Can Students Work While Studying in Malta?

Some international students may be allowed to work in Malta, but this depends on the type of course, residence status, and official conditions. Identita guidance says some students on courses exceeding 90 days may take up employment under conditions, and students may be limited to a maximum of 20 hours per week depending on their situation.

You can review the official Identita student employment guidance before making financial plans.

Do not depend on part-time work to pay for your full study plan. Work permission is not automatic for every student, and job availability can depend on language level, schedule, location, and employer needs.

FAQ

What are the basic requirements to study in Malta?

The basic requirements usually include a valid passport, course acceptance, academic documents, proof of funds, accommodation proof, insurance, and visa documents if required.

Do I need IELTS to study in Malta?

Not always. English language schools may use a placement test, while universities and colleges may request IELTS or another accepted English proof. Requirements depend on the institution and course.

Do I need a visa to study in Malta?

It depends on your nationality and course duration. Non-EU students may need a short-stay C-visa for courses up to 90 days or a long-stay D-visa for courses exceeding 90 days.

How much money do I need to show for a Malta student visa?

The amount can depend on your stay length, accommodation, and visa route. Students should prepare clear bank statements or sponsor documents and check current official requirements before applying.

Can I work while studying in Malta?

Some students may be allowed to work under specific conditions, but work permission depends on course type, residence status, and official rules. Always check the latest guidance before relying on part-time work.
